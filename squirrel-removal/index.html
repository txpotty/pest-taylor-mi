Our Comprehensive Squirrel Removal Process

Our approach to squirrel removal is thorough and designed to eliminate current infestations while also preventing future issues. We follow a systematic process to ensure the best possible outcome for your home or business in Taylor.

Initial Inspection and Assessment

The first step is always a detailed inspection of your property. Our technicians will carefully examine your home's exterior, attic, crawl spaces, and any other areas where squirrels might be active. We look for signs of entry, nesting sites, and the extent of the infestation. This allows us to develop a targeted and residential or commercial removal plan.

Identifying Entry Points

Understanding how squirrels are getting into your property is paramount. Common entry points include gaps in roofing, damaged siding, uncapped chimneys, vents, and even small openings around windows and doors. Our technicians are skilled at spotting these vulnerable areas, which are often not obvious to the untrained eye. Without addressing the entry points, squirrel problems are likely to return, making this step crucial for long-term prevention.

Safe and Humane Squirrel Removal

Once we've assessed the situation, we implement safe and effective removal strategies. The method used will depend on the specific circumstances of the infestation.

Setting Traps

In many cases, humane live traps are the most appropriate method for capturing squirrels. These traps are designed to safely capture the animal without causing harm. We place traps in strategic locations where squirrel activity is observed, such as near entry points or nesting areas. Regular monitoring of traps is essential to ensure the well-being of captured animals.

Excluding from Structures

For situations where squirrels are actively using a specific entry point, we may employ exclusion techniques. This involves sealing the entry point once the squirrels have left the structure, effectively preventing them from re-entering. This method is often used in conjunction with trapping to ensure all individuals are removed before sealing.

Preventative Measures and Repair

Removal is only part of the solution. To truly solve your squirrel problem, preventative measures are essential. We offer services to repair damage caused by squirrels and reinforce potential entry points.

Sealing Entry Points and Repairs

Once squirrels are removed, we seal all identified entry points using durable materials that squirrels cannot easily chew through. This might involve repairing damaged fascia boards, sealing gaps around vents and chimneys, and reinforcing screens. Addressing these vulnerabilities is critical to preventing future infestations and protecting your investment in your property.

Common Squirrel Problems and Why You Need Professional Squirrel Removal

Squirrels might seem like cute backyard creatures, but when they decide to make your home their home, they can become a serious nuisance and cause significant damage. Understanding the common issues associated with squirrel infestations can help you recognize the signs and understand the importance of seeking professional help promptly. Ignoring a squirrel problem can lead to escalating damage and increased costs in the long run. Many homeowners in Wyandotte, Dearborn, and other neighboring areas also face similar challenges with nuisance wildlife invading their properties. Our expertise extends beyond just squirrel removal; we also offer comprehensive raccoon removal and a variety of rodent control services to address a wide range of pest challenges.

Chewing on Electrical Wiring

One of the most dangerous problems caused by squirrels is their propensity to chew on electrical wiring. Their incisors grow continuously, making chewing a necessary activity for them to keep their teeth trimmed. Attics and wall voids are prime locations for electrical wiring, and squirrels see them as convenient chewing materials. Damaged wiring creates a significant fire hazard, potentially putting your home and family at risk. This is not an issue to be taken lightly, and professional intervention is needed to assess the damage and safely remove the threat.

Damaged Insulation and Structural Components

Squirrels often build nests in attics and wall cavities, using insulation as nesting material. This can lead to compacted or destroyed insulation, significantly reducing its effectiveness and leading to increased energy bills. Beyond insulation, squirrels can also gnaw on wooden beams, rafters, and other structural components, weakening the integrity of your home over time. The longer squirrels are allowed to inhabit these areas, the more extensive the damage becomes, potentially requiring costly repairs.

Noise and Disturbances

The sounds of scratching, scurrying, and chirping squirrels in your walls or ceiling can be incredibly disruptive and unsettling. This constant noise can interfere with sleep and create an uncomfortable living environment. As nocturnal or crepuscular animals (most active during dawn and dusk), their activity often peaks when you are trying to relax or sleep. The sounds are not only annoying but also a clear indicator that you have uninvited guests residing within your home's structure. Professional squirrel removal can quickly eliminate these disturbances and restore quiet to your home.

Contamination and Health Risks

Squirrel droppings and urine can accumulate in nesting areas, creating unsanitary conditions. This waste can harbor bacteria and parasites, posing potential health risks to humans. The odor can also become quite strong and seep into living areas. Furthermore, squirrels can carry fleas and ticks, which can then transfer to your pets and even into your home, creating additional pest issues. Addressing a squirrel infestation promptly is crucial for maintaining a healthy environment within your home and preventing the spread of potential diseases or secondary pest infestations like flea treatment or tick control.

Damage to Gardens and Outdoor Features

Beyond invading your home's structure, squirrels can also be a significant nuisance in your yard and garden. They are notorious for digging up flower bulbs, eating vegetables and fruits, and damaging bird feeders. They can also gnaw on outdoor furniture, decks, and fences. While some garden damage might seem minor, repeated squirrel activity can destroy carefully cultivated landscapes and outdoor living spaces. Protecting your outdoor investment from these persistent foragers often requires professional intervention.

Signs You Need Professional Squirrel Removal

Recognizing the signs of a squirrel infestation early can save you from significant damage and stress. Be on the lookout for the following indicators:

  • Scratching or Scurrying Sounds: Hearing noises coming from your attic, walls, or ceiling, especially in the morning or evening. These sounds are often the most obvious sign of squirrel activity within your home's structure.
  • Visible Damage: Noticeable chewing marks on wood, wiring, insulation, or other materials within your home or on the exterior of your property. Look for shredded insulation in your attic or gnawed wood trim.
  • Entry Points: Finding gaps or holes in your roofline, siding, vents, or chimney caps. These are the highways squirrels use to get into your home.
  • Nesting Materials: Discovering accumulations of leaves, twigs, and other debris in your attic or other areas, indicating a nesting site.
  • Increased Squirrel Activity: Observing a higher than usual number of squirrels on or around your property, particularly near your home's entry points.
  • Unpleasant Odors: Detecting a strong, musty odor in your home, which can be caused by squirrel urine and droppings.

If you notice any of these signs, it's important to act quickly. Attempting to remove squirrels yourself can be dangerous and often ineffective, leading to further damage and frustration. Professional squirrel removal services have the expertise, equipment, and knowledge to safely and effectively handle the situation. They can identify hidden entry points and implement long-term solutions to prevent future problems. Beyond squirrels, these signs can also overlap with issues caused by other nuisance wildlife, highlighting the need for experienced wildlife control professionals.
